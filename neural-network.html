2. Case Study: The Circle Problem

Using the TensorFlow Playground, I trained a model to separate a complex dataset (a circle of blue dots surrounded by a ring of orange dots). Because this data is circular, traditional linear machine learning models fail instantly. Here is how the Neural Network solved it.

Understanding the Results

1. Why 0 Hidden Layers Fails (The Scissors Analogy): A neural network with no hidden layers can only draw a single straight line through the data. Imagine trying to cut a perfect circle out of paper using only one straight cut with scissors—it’s impossible. You need multiple cuts intersecting to carve out a shape. That is why hidden layers are mandatory for complex data.

2. Why "4x2" Architecture Worked: By putting 4 neurons in the first layer, we gave the model 4 "scissors." It learned to draw a box (4 straight lines) around the inner circle. The next layer of 2 neurons then took that box and smoothed the sharp corners, resulting in a perfect circular boundary.

3. The Magic of Tanh (Activation): If you just add neurons together, they still only make straight lines. The Tanh activation function is the "curve maker." It applies a mathematical curve to the data passing through the neurons, allowing the model to bend the straight lines into the circular shape required to solve this problem.

4. Optimization & Learning Rate (The Blindfolded Hiker): Optimization (Gradient Descent) is like a blindfolded hiker trying to find the bottom of a valley. The "Loss" is their current altitude. The Learning Rate (0.03) is the size of the steps they take. At 0.03, the hiker took careful, steady steps to the bottom.
