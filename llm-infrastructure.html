1. What is an LLM doing?

At its core, a Generative AI model like ChatGPT or Gemini is essentially a highly advanced autocomplete engine. It doesn't "think" like a human; instead, it uses massive amounts of mathematical probability to guess the most logical next word (token) in a sentence based on the billions of documents it has read.

4. The Core Resource Pillars

The foundational requirements for building generative artificial intelligence at scale.

💻 Computational Power (Compute)

Training requires clusters of specialized AI accelerators (NVIDIA H100s, Google TPUs) running continuous matrix multiplications. The bottleneck is often the "interconnect" speed—how fast these GPUs can pass data back and forth across the network.

⚡ Energy & Cooling

AI clusters consume staggering electricity. Training a single frontier model uses tens of thousands of Megawatt-hours (MWh). Datacenters must also invest heavily in advanced liquid cooling infrastructure to prevent hardware meltdown.

🗄️ Data Curation

Models ingest petabytes of text. However, "dirty" data creates bad models. Significant human and automated resources are spent filtering out PII (Personal Identifiable Information), toxic content, and duplicate web scrapes.

⏳ Time & Iteration

Beyond the months required for Pre-Training, immense time is spent on Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF). This requires paying thousands of human experts to manually rank AI responses to teach the model safety and alignment.

Insights & Learning

Creating this interactive web artifact fundamentally shifted my perspective on generative artificial intelligence. Coming from a background in traditional data analytics and engineering, it is easy to view AI models simply as advanced software layers or statistical algorithms. This exercise forced me to confront the immense physical, financial, and environmental realities required to train these models from scratch.

One of the primary challenges I encountered during this assignment was distilling the sheer magnitude of these operations—tens of thousands of GPUs, millions of dollars, and petabytes of data—into a format that wouldn't overwhelm a non-technical audience. To solve this, I employed the guided walkthrough design to pace the information, pairing highly technical infrastructure data with accessible, plain-English analogies to prevent cognitive overload.

This project highlighted a critical business reality that will directly inform my future career in AI: the barrier to entry for training frontier models is insurmountable for most enterprises. Understanding this scale reinforces the strategic importance of leveraging pre-trained open-source models and utilizing resource-efficient architectures like RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) or targeted fine-tuning to deliver business value without incurring $100M+ compute costs.
