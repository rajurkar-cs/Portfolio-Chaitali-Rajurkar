How Generative AI is Built

Demystifying the training pipeline, infrastructure, and true costs of Large Language Models (LLMs).

1. What is an LLM doing?

At its core, a Generative AI model like ChatGPT or Gemini is essentially a highly advanced autocomplete engine. It doesn't "think" like a human; instead, it uses massive amounts of mathematical probability to guess the most logical next word in a sentence.

However, teaching a computer to accurately guess the next word in thousands of different languages, coding frameworks, and logical scenarios requires an unimaginable amount of data, electricity, and supercomputers. We call this the Training Pipeline.

AI models are fed "Trillions of Tokens" (a token is roughly a piece of a word). Developers scrape the entire public internet, Wikipedia, and digitized books. The hardest part is filtering—using algorithms to remove toxic content, personal information (PII), and low-quality spam.

Pre-Training (The Core Burn)

This is where the massive cost comes in. The AI is loaded onto a cluster of tens of thousands of specialized GPUs (like NVIDIA H100s). It processes the data over and over, continually adjusting its internal math (parameters) to get better at predicting text. This takes months of non-stop computing.

💡 The Beginner Analogy:
The person now knows everything, but they talk like a chaotic encyclopedia. Fine-tuning is like sending them to finishing school. Human teachers grade their answers, rewarding them for being polite, helpful, and safe, and punishing them for lying or being toxic.

Fine-Tuning (SFT & RLHF)

A base model is useless to a consumer. It must undergo Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) and Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF). Thousands of human experts manually rank the AI's responses to teach it to follow instructions (like "write a poem") instead of just spitting out random web text.

Deployment & Inference

Once training is done, the model is deployed. Inference is the act of the model generating a response to a user's prompt. While training is a massive one-time cost, inference is an ongoing cost. Every single time a user hits "send" on ChatGPT, a GPU in a datacenter somewhere has to perform math to generate the words.

3. Real-World Infrastructure Requirements

Here is a look at the estimated physical resources required by the tech giants to build the models we use today.

GPT-4

OpenAI

  • Compute: ~25,000 NVIDIA A100 GPUs
  • Data: Multiple Trillions of Tokens
  • Est. Cost: $100M+ USD

LLaMA 3

Meta (Open Source)

  • Compute: Two custom 24k H100 clusters
  • Data: 15 Trillion Tokens
  • Est. Cost: Hardware highly capitalized internally

Gemini 1.5

Google

  • Compute: Custom TPUv4 / TPUv5e Pods
  • Data: Native Multimodal (Text, Video, Audio)
  • Est. Cost: Proprietary internal cost

Claude 3

Anthropic

  • Compute: Massive AWS / GCP Clusters
  • Focus: Heavy investment in Constitutional AI / RLHF
  • Est. Cost: ~$100M+ USD
